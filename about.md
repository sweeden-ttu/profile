# About Me

Originally from Oregon, I moved to Texas a year ago to study computer science at Texas Tech University. While completing my degree, I plan to study abroad for a semester in Australia to find new challenges and opportunities in machine learning and its applications in solving complex social, environmental, and healthcare issues.

## Academic Journey

During my studies at Texas Tech, I have so far:

- **Authored an IEEE peer review article** of a Microsoft Research publication, contributing to the academic discourse in machine learning research methodologies
- **Perfected the Master's theorem** in algorithm analysis, deepening my understanding of recursive algorithm complexity and divide-and-conquer approaches
- **Participated in Kaggle machine learning competitions**, applying theoretical knowledge to practical, real-world datasets
- **Published YouTube video shorts on reinforcement learning**, making complex AI concepts accessible to broader audiences
- **Created a propositional logic utility** for Chrome extensions, bridging formal logic with practical web development
- **Published a Q-Learning Pac-Man demonstration** on GitHub, showcasing reinforcement learning in action

## Research Interests

My primary research focus centers on machine learning applications that address meaningful challenges:

- **Social Impact**: Using ML to understand and solve societal problems through data-driven insights
- **Environmental Applications**: Applying computational methods to environmental monitoring and sustainability
- **Healthcare Solutions**: Exploring how algorithms can improve healthcare outcomes and accessibility
- **Algorithm Analysis**: Understanding the theoretical foundations that make efficient ML possible

## Beyond Academia

An avid biker, I enjoy weekend tours in the Dallas, Austin, and San Antonio areas. The long rides provide time for reflection and often spark new ideas for research projects. I still have a passion for travel and hope to develop new international business and research connections.

I'm always open to new experiences, cultures, and ways of thinking. This openness extends to my academic work—I believe the best solutions emerge when we integrate diverse perspectives and interdisciplinary approaches.

## Looking Forward

My goal is to contribute to machine learning research that makes a tangible difference in people's lives. Whether that's through more efficient algorithms, novel applications of existing techniques, or entirely new approaches to long-standing problems, I'm committed to work that matters.

I'm particularly interested in opportunities for international collaboration, especially as I prepare for my semester abroad in Australia. If you're working on interesting problems at the intersection of ML and real-world impact, I'd love to connect.
