## Section 2: What Is Logic? (400 words)

**Definition**: Logic is a method of reasoning, the study of proving or disproving arguments, and a method of making inferences.

**Key Points**:
- Historical context: From Aristotle to modern computing
- The three core aspects of logic: reasoning, proof, inference
- Distinction between formal and informal reasoning

**Examples**:
1. Peter and John parent example (shows logical inference)
2. Socrates syllogism (classical deductive reasoning)
3. London in Europe (transitive property)

**Visual Ideas**:
- Mermaid flowchart showing logical inference process
- Comparison table: Formal vs Informal reasoning

## Section 4: Logic in Computer Science (450 words)

**Four Major Applications**:

1. **Computer Architecture**: How logic gates (AND, OR, NOT) form the building blocks of processors
2. **Programming Principles**: Boolean logic, conditionals, loops, and algorithmic thinking
3. **Databases**: Relational algebra, SQL queries, selection and projection operations
4. **Artificial Intelligence**: Knowledge representation, automated reasoning, logic-based AI agents

**Visual Ideas**:
- Mermaid diagram: From Logic Gates to CPU Architecture
- Table comparing logical operations to programming constructs
- Concept map: Logic across computer science domains
