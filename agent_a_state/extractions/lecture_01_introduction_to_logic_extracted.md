## Main Content: Introduction to Logic

### What is Logic?

**Basic Definitions:**
- A method of reasoning
- Study of proving or disproving arguments
- A method of making inferences

### Example from Lecture

**Parent Relationship Example:**
> "Assume that for any two persons A and B, A is a parent of B if A is the father of B or A is the mother of B. Given that Peter is the father of John. Question: Is Peter a parent of John?"

**Socrates Example:**
> Given: "All men are mortal" and "Socrates is a man"
> Conclusion: "Socrates is mortal"

### London in Europe Example
> "London is in England and England is in Europe. Therefore, London is in Europe."

## Why Study Logic in Computer Science?

1. **Computer Architecture**: At an abstract level, logic concepts govern the operation of electrical circuits that make up a computer

2. **Programming Principles**: Understanding of logic is fundamental to programming

3. **Databases**: Logic lies at the core of relational algebra (selection, projection, etc.)

4. **Artificial Intelligence**: Building "intelligent" agents is based on logic-based approaches

## Key Concepts for Further Development

### Propositional Logic
- Study of propositions (statements that are true or false)
- Logical connectives (and, or, not, implies, etc.)
- Truth tables for evaluating compound propositions

### Predicate Logic
- Extends propositional logic with quantifiers
- Deals with predicates and functions
- Foundation for formal reasoning about properties

### Resolutions
- Rule of inference for automated theorem proving
- Basis for logic programming (Prolog)

### Herbrand Logic
- Foundation for understanding logical consequence
- Important for automated reasoning systems
