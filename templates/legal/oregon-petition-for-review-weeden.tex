Now comes Scott Douglas Weeden of Multnomah County, Oregon, appearing pro se, and respectfully petitions this Court for review.

\section*{Questions Presented for Review}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\arabic*., leftmargin=1.2em, itemsep=0.25\baselineskip]
  \item By citation of a case which remains sealed and refusing the disclosure of further Quack, has the Court of Appeals sufficiently answered the defendants request to know my accuser?
  \item Upon appeal has it been pointed to another case which questionable evidence was used in the taking of other private property without compensation?
  \item Should the practice be allowed to continue in the Multnomah Circuit court without any punishment for the very (un)professional acts by which attorneys have entered this evidence during secret hearings?
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Concise Statement in Support of Review}
It is no secret and it has become quite the fashion of the justices and attorneys which abuse this process in the Multnomah Circuit court to bring upon the court whenever convenience suits, rather than at summons when defendants appear, questionable evidence against those accused and enter it to the record while simultaneously refusing to provide the names or information of the accusations which have been entered against the defendants.

In the Court of Appeals Opinion which was published November 26th, it was stated that while disagreement of the opinion between the Three Honorable Justices could not be reached, and as to if this evidence alone could be determined by a reasonable doubt it was allowed to be considered in the first place, I have vocally objected to and provided ample opportunity for those who might wish to make their accusations known, not only upon summons but upon appearance at trial and hearings both in appeal and in person so that I may face my accuser. That accuser has chosen to remain anonymous and I am without property which has been taken without compensation.

The opinion continues from that point and states that which was the authority for the court which was used by the Municipal Court was the Oregon Trust Deed Act Code. I do not perceive that Honorable Justice Egan in his opinion has ceded our Federal guidelines and would use Oregon Law for which it cannot override the Fifth Amendment. Honorable Justice Egan , by his citation of another Appeal which was not mine, he has pointed out the further abuses that which Multnomah Circuit Court have abused too many times in the taking of personal property without the proper handling of evidence.

It should seem to me that the appropriate professional understanding would have been, that a justice which were \emph{temporere} would have stepped down rather than take another seat by which he should be less trustworthy of the players involved this time. Because it was only upon appeal I was able to point to the evidence which showed a concerted effort by the Plaintiff to avoid appearance at summons and then conceal business ties between the law firm and the trustee with which that evidence was entered against me. Given these facts in its entirety I must question if in Egan's strongest opinion I must also request he suspension or penalties against the attorneys who continue serious professional infractions. There could be no more serious of consequences which my family and others are suffering at the lax and unprofessional handling of evidence entered on record and then held in secret for any amount of time. So it was my last and most loudest objection that I am praying for relief, and myself Scott Weeden seeking that this most honorable court put to rest the use of Latin phrases ``ex parte'' which should not excuse the serious violations of the Oregon Tenth Amendment, that which prohibits secret trials and provide relief in the matter as it see fit.

\section*{Relief Requested}
I am praying for relief, and I seek that this most honorable court provide relief in the matter as it see fit.
